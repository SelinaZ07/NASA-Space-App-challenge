Space Biology Knowledge Engine

About Our Project

Welcome to the NASA Biology Hub! NASA has been performing biology experiments in space for decades, generating a tremendous amount of information that will need to be considered as humans prepare to revisit the Moon and explore Mars. Although this knowledge is publicly available, it can be difficult for potential users to find information that pertains to their specific interests. Therefore, this is summarizes all the 608 research papers on Space Biology publications from Nasa, into 10 categories using machine learning models. We also leveraged Artificial Intelligence to organize all the research's finding, purpose and result for better understanding. Additionally, we built an AI assistant that can help you with specific questions you have regarding Nasa's space biology research. We also used the existing date to build visualizations that help user identify trends, patterns, knowledge gaps, and emerging areas of interest in space biology research.
